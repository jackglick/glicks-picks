How It Works

Every pitch tells a story

The models analyze four years of Statcast data to predict pitcher and batter outcomes tonight.

01

Crunch the pitches

Every pitch from 2022–2025 — velocity, movement, location, outcome. That's the raw material for engineered features capturing pitcher tendencies, batter matchups, and park effects.

02

Know the matchup

Who's batting? How often do they swing and miss? What's the park factor? Who's behind the plate calling strikes? It all matters.

03

Measure the gap

Each model predicts outcomes, converts to probabilities, and compares to the sportsbook line. When the gap is big enough — the model flags it.

Why player props?

Game-level markets (totals, moneyline, run lines) are efficiently priced — the models confirmed this through extensive testing. But player prop markets have lower liquidity and less sophisticated pricing. That's where the edge lives: the gap between predicted outcomes and what sportsbooks are offering.

About this project

Built by Jack Glick, a data scientist and lifelong baseball fan. This started as a personal project to see whether machine learning could find meaningful signal in MLB player props — and grew into a seven-market system covering pitcher and batter outcomes.

This is a research project, not a picks service. The models are trained on four years of Statcast pitch-level data and evaluated on out-of-sample holdout seasons with real sportsbook odds. All statistics shown on this site come from backtesting — not live wagering.
