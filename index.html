How It Works

Every pitch tells a story

The model reads three years of Statcast data to predict what a pitcher will do tonight.

01

Crunch the pitches

Every pitch from 2022–2024 — velocity, movement, location, outcome. That's the raw material for 14 custom features per start.

02

Know the matchup

Who's batting? How often do they swing and miss? What's the park factor? Who's behind the plate calling strikes? It all matters.

03

Measure the gap

The model predicts strikeouts, converts to probabilities, and compares to the sportsbook line. When the gap is big enough — the model flags it.

Larger model-vs-market gaps predict better outcomes

Bigger disagreements between the model and the market mean higher hit rates.

What's "edge"?

The gap between what the model thinks will happen and what the sportsbook is pricing. A 5% edge means the model sees a significantly better chance of hitting than the odds imply. That gap is what the model looks for.

About this project

Built by Jack Glick, a data scientist and lifelong baseball fan. This started as a personal project to see whether machine learning could find meaningful signal in MLB strikeout props.

This is a research project, not a picks service. The model is trained on Statcast pitch-level data and evaluated on out-of-sample holdout seasons. All statistics shown on this site come from backtesting — not live wagering.
